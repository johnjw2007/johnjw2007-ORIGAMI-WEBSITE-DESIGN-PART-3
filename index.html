Origami Camel

A camel is an even-toed ungulate in the genus Camelus that bears distinctive fatty deposits known as "humps" on its back. Camels have long been domesticated and, as livestock, they provide food and textiles

Origami Lion

The lion is a species in the family Felidae and a member of the genus Panthera. It has a muscular, deep-chested body, short, rounded head, round ears, and a hairy tuft at the end of its tail.

Origami Alpaca

The alpaca is a species of South American camelid mammal. However, alpacas are often noticeably smaller than llamas. The two animals are closely related and can successfully crossbreed.

Origami Kangaroo

The kangaroo is a marsupial from the family Macropodidae.The red kangaroo, as well as the antilopine kangaroo. Kangaroos are indigenous to Australia and New Guinea.

Origami Panda

The panda, is a bear native to South Central China. It is characterised by its bold black-and-white coat and rotund body. The name "gaint panda" is sometimes used to distinguish it from the red panda.

Origami Sheep

Sheep are quadrupedal, ruminant mammals typically kept as livestock. Like all ruminants, sheep are members of the order Artiodactyla, the even-toed ungulates.

Origami Giraffe

The giraffe is an African artiodactyl mammal, the tallest living terrestrial animal and the largest ruminant. It is traditionally considered to be one species, Giraffa camelopardalis, with nine subspecies.
